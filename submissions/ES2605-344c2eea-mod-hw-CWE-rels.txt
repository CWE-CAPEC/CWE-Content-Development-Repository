NAME: Add relationships to certain hardware CWEs

DESCRIPTION:

Background - Through a recent third-party IP (3PIP) assurance project,
we have reviewed HW CWEs in extensive detail and identified several
overlaps.

Source of Confusion - The root cause for many CWEs ended up being
interpreted to be the same, which resulted in re-use of verification
data for multiple CWEs.

Recommendation - Possible improvements to the Relationship entries to
clarify these similarities.

CWE entries can have relationship notes that we can use to compare and
contrast CWEs. For CWE-1239's CanAlsoBe relationships - and possibly
others - relationship notes could be used to clarify so people won't
pick the wrong CWE.


Recommendations
---------------

1) CWE-1220: Insufficient Granularity of Access Control

  CWE-1262, CWE-1299, and CWE-1222

  Make all ChildOf of CWE-1220 (CWE-1222 is already a child of CWE-1220)

  There was a lot of discussion on this one since this deals with
  Access Control.

2) CWE-1299: Missing Protection Mechanism for Alternate Hardware Interface

  Create a PeerOf relationship between CWE-1262 and CWE-1299

3) CWE-1303: Non-Transparent Sharing of Microarchitectural Resources

  Add PeerOf relationship between CWE-1303 and CWE-1421

4) CWE-1272: Sensitive Information Uncleared Before Debug/Power State
Transition

  - Make CWE-1258 a ChildOf CWE-1272

  - Add CWE-1239 a CanAlsoBe relationship to both CWE-1272 and CWE-1258

5) CWE-1240: Use of a Cryptographic Primitive with a Risky Implementation

  - Make CWE-1431 a ChildOf CWE-325

  - Make CWE-1431 a PeerOf CWE-1420 and CWE-1258
